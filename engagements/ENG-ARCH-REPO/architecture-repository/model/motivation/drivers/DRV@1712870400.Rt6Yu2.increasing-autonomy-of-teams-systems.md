## Increasing Autonomy of Teams & Systems

Modern organizational design favors greater autonomy for teams and subsystems — accepting higher failure rates in exchange for increased innovation velocity and iteration speed (cf. contingency theory). However, organizations that achieve integration (shared understanding of goals, values, and requirements; reusable learnings) even through increased diversification will outperform those that cannot. The architecture repository must support this balance: enabling autonomous work within engagements while preserving enterprise-wide coherence through the two-tiered repository structure and promotion mechanisms.
